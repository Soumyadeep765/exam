## Topic 1: Summer Exam Season & Railway Recruitment

### Summary
May 2026 is peak season for **government exam notifications and CBT schedules**. RRB NTPC and other railway recruitment activities intensify.

### Important Facts
- RRB NTPC CEN cycles follow multi-year patterns
- CBT 1 format: 100 Q, 90 min, 3 sections
- Admit cards issued ~4 days before exam
- Normalization applied across shifts
- Document verification follows CBT stages

### Possible NTPC Questions

**Q1. RRB NTPC CBT 1 has a duration of:**
- A) 60 minutes
- B) 75 minutes
- C) 90 minutes
- D) 120 minutes

**Answer: C**

**Q2. Negative marking in RRB NTPC is:**
- A) 1/4 mark per wrong answer
- B) 1/3 mark per wrong answer
- C) 1/2 mark per wrong answer
- D) 1 mark per wrong answer

**Answer: B**

## Topic 2: Heatwave & Disaster Management

### Summary
May typically sees severe **heatwaves** across North and Central India. NDMA (National Disaster Management Authority) guidelines and heat action plans activated.

### Important Facts
- NDMA: apex body for disaster management in India
- NDMA Chairman: **Prime Minister of India**
- Heat Action Plans in 23 states
- IMD (India Meteorological Department) heatwave alerts
- NDRF (National Disaster Response Force) on standby

### Possible NTPC Questions

**Q3. NDMA Chairman is:**
- A) Home Minister
- B) Prime Minister
- C) Defence Minister
- D) Environment Minister

**Answer: B**

**Q4. NDRF stands for:**
- A) National Disaster Relief Force
- B) National Disaster Response Force
- C) National Development and Recovery Fund
- D) National Defence Response Force

**Answer: B**

**Q5. IMD is headquartered at:**
- A) Mumbai
- B) New Delhi
- C) Pune
- D) Kolkata

**Answer: C** (Pune; operational HQ New Delhi)

## Topic 3: Indian Railways Updates

### Summary
Railway-specific current affairs highly relevant for NTPC. Focus on new trains, Vande Bharat expansion, station redevelopment, and safety initiatives.

### Important Facts
- **Vande Bharat Express:** indigenous semi-high-speed train
- **Amrit Bharat Station Scheme:** 1,309 stations to be redeveloped
- **Kavach:** indigenous automatic train protection system
- Railway zones: **19 zones** (including Konkan Railway)
- Railway Board Chairman heads administration
- First train in India: **1853** (Mumbai to Thane, 16 April)
- Longest platform: **Gorakhpur**, Uttar Pradesh

### Possible NTPC Questions

**Q6. India's first train ran between:**
- A) Delhi and Agra
- B) Mumbai and Thane
- C) Kolkata and Howrah
- D) Chennai and Bangalore

**Answer: B** (16 April 1853)

**Q7. Kavach is:**
- A) A new train model
- B) An automatic train protection system
- C) A railway station scheme
- D) A freight corridor project

**Answer: B**

**Q8. How many railway zones are there in India (including Konkan Railway)?**
- A) 16
- B) 17
- C) 18
- D) 19

**Answer: D**
