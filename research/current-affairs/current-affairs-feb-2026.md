## Topic 1: Union Budget 2026–27

### Summary
Finance Minister **Nirmala Sitharaman** presented the **Union Budget 2026–27** on **1 February 2026**. The budget emphasized asset creation, infrastructure, and fiscal consolidation.

### Important Facts
- Total expenditure: **₹53,47,315 crore** (7.7% increase over RE 2025–26)
- Total receipts (excluding borrowings): **₹36,51,547 crore** (7.2% increase)
- Nominal GDP growth assumed: **11%** for 2026–27
- Capital expenditure focus: roads, railways, defence, semiconductors
- Income tax: No tax up to **₹12 lakh** income (announced in President's Address)
- Finance Bill 2026 introduced alongside Budget

### Possible NTPC Questions

**Q1. Who presented the Union Budget 2026–27?**
- A) Prime Minister Narendra Modi
- B) Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman
- C) RBI Governor
- D) Chief Economic Advisor

**Answer: B**

**Q2. The estimated total expenditure in Union Budget 2026–27 is approximately:**
- A) ₹40 lakh crore
- B) ₹45 lakh crore
- C) ₹53.47 lakh crore
- D) ₹60 lakh crore

**Answer: C**

**Q3. On which date was Union Budget 2026–27 presented?**
- A) 26 January 2026
- B) 1 February 2026
- C) 15 February 2026
- D) 1 March 2026

**Answer: B**

### Important Facts

## Topic 3: Industrial Relations Code Amendment 2026

### Summary
A Bill amending the **Industrial Relations Code, 2020** was introduced and passed during the Budget Session. It aims to streamline labour regulations and improve ease of doing business.

### Important Facts
- Part of labour code reforms (4 codes: IR, Social Security, Occupational Safety, Wages)
- Passed by both Houses during Budget Session 2026
- Focus: fixed-term employment, dispute resolution, trade union recognition

- Focus: rural infrastructure and asset creation
