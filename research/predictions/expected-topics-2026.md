## 1. Prediction Methodology

Each prediction uses a weighted scoring model:

| Factor | Weight | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Historical Frequency | 40% | How often topic appeared (2021–2025) |
| Recency Momentum | 30% | Trend direction (rising/stable/declining) |
| Syllabus Alignment | 20% | Official RRB syllabus coverage |
| External Triggers | 10% | Events in 2025–2026 boosting topic likelihood |

**Score Interpretation:**
- **90–100:** Almost certain (appears in every shift)
- **75–89:** Highly likely (appears in 80%+ shifts)
- **60–74:** Likely (appears in 60%+ shifts)
- **40–59:** Possible (appears occasionally)
- **Below 40:** Low probability

## 2. Most Likely Mathematics Topics

| Rank | Topic | Prediction Score | Expected Q | Reasoning |
|------|-------|-----------------|------------|-----------|
| 1 | **Simplification/BODMAS** | 97 | 4–5 | Appeared in 100% of 2025 shifts. Easy marks, time-efficient. Always present. |
| 2 | **Profit & Loss & Discount** | 94 | 3–4 | Increasing trend (2023→2025). Successive discount variants common. |
| 3 | **Percentage, Ratio & Proportion** | 93 | 4–5 | Combined questions frequent. Foundational arithmetic — never skipped. |
| 4 | **Number System (LCM/HCF)** | 91 | 3–4 | Stable high frequency. Word problems with LCM×HCF product rule. |
| 5 | **Time, Speed & Distance** | 89 | 2–3 | Boat-stream and train problems in every 2025 shift. Stable high priority. |
| 6 | **Simple & Compound Interest** | 85 | 2–3 | CI-SI difference type repeated across years. Formula-based scoring. |
| 7 | **Mensuration** | 82 | 2–3 | Circle, cylinder, cone formulae tested in 2025. Geometry crossover. |
| 8 | **Data Interpretation** | 78 | 1–2 | Emerging trend — appeared in CBT 1 from 2023 onward. Bar/pie charts. |
| 9 | **Time & Work** | 72 | 1–2 | Declining frequency but still appears. Pipe-cistern sub-type occasional. |
| 10 | **Statistics (Mean/Median/Mode)** | 70 | 1 | Easy free marks. Appeared in 2025 shifts. Low prep, high ROI. |
| 11 | Algebra & Trigonometry | 58 | 1–2 | Basic level only. Not deep — identity-based questions. |
| 12 | Pipe & Cistern | 55 | 0–1 | Occasional. Often combined with Time & Work. |

### Math Prediction Reasoning

**Why Simplification is #1:** Every single shift from 2021–2025 had 3–5 simplification questions. They are easy, fast, and carry zero risk if BODMAS rules are mastered.

**Why Profit & Loss is rising:** From 2 Q (2021) → 3 Q (2023) → 3–4 Q (2025). RRB increasingly tests multi-step P/L chains (CP → MP → Discount → Profit%).

**Why DI is emerging:** Previously CBT 2 only. Now appearing in CBT 1 (1–2 Q since 2023). Expect this to continue as RRB tests data literacy.
