## Q1. Simplification (2025 Shift Pattern)

**Question:**  
What is the value of  
`18 + 12 ÷ 6 × 4 − 5 × 2 + 8`?

- A) 15  
- B) 17  
- C) 19  
- D) 21  

**Answer: B) 17**

**Explanation:**  
Apply BODMAS — Division & Multiplication left to right, then +/−.

| Step | Calculation |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | 12 ÷ 6 = **2** |
| 2 | 2 × 4 = **8** |
| 3 | 5 × 2 = **10** |
| 4 | 18 + 8 − 10 + 8 = **17** |

## Q2. Simplification (Nested Brackets)

**Question:**  
Find the value of:  
`42 − [18 ÷ {3 × (6 − 4)} + 7]`

- A) 28  
- B) 30  
- C) 32  
- D) 34  

**Answer: C) 32**

**Explanation:**

| Step | Calculation |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | (6 − 4) = **2** |
| 2 | 3 × 2 = **6** |
| 3 | 18 ÷ 6 = **3** |
| 4 | 3 + 7 = **10** |
| 5 | 42 − 10 = **32** |

Always solve **innermost bracket first**, then outward.

## Q3. Profit & Loss (Marked Price Chain — Very Common in 2025)

**Question:**  
A shopkeeper marks his goods **40% above** the cost price and allows a discount of **20%** on the marked price. What is his **profit percentage**?

- A) 10%  
- B) 12%  
- C) 15%  
- D) 20%  

**Answer: B) 12%**

**Explanation:**  
Let CP = ₹100 (easy method).

| Step | Value |
|------|-------|
| Marked Price | 100 + 40% = **₹140** |
| Discount 20% on MP | 140 × 0.80 = **₹112** |
| Profit | 112 − 100 = ₹12 |
| Profit % | (12/100) × 100 = **12%** |

**Trap:** 40% − 20% ≠ 20% profit. Never add/subtract percentages directly on different bases.

## Q4. Successive Discount (June 2025 Type)

**Question:**  
The marked price of a watch is ₹2,500. The shopkeeper gives two successive discounts of **10%** and **20%**. What is the **selling price**?

- A) ₹1,750  
- B) ₹1,800  
- C) ₹1,850  
- D) ₹1,900  

**Answer: B) ₹1,800**

**Explanation:**

**Method 1 — Step by step:**  
- After 10% discount: 2500 × 0.90 = ₹2,250  
- After 20% on 2250: 2250 × 0.80 = **₹1,800**

**Method 2 — Net discount formula:**  
Net discount = 10 + 20 − (10×20)/100 = **28%**  
SP = 2500 × 0.72 = **₹1,800**

## Q5. Percentage — Population Growth (Repeated Pattern)

**Question:**  
The population of a town is **80,000**. It increases by **15%** in the first year and by **10%** in the second year. What is the population after 2 years?

- A) 96,000  
- B) 99,200  
- C) 1,01,200  
- D) 1,04,000  

**Answer: C) 1,01,200**

**Explanation:**

| Year | Calculation | Population |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Start | — | 80,000 |
| After +15% | 80,000 × 1.15 | 92,000 |
| After +10% | 92,000 × 1.10 | **1,01,200** |

**Shortcut:** Net % = 15 + 10 + (15×10)/100 = **26.5%**  
80,000 × 1.265 = **1,01,200**

## Q7. Simple & Compound Interest — Difference Type (2025 Asked)

**Question:**  
What is the difference between the compound interest and simple interest on **₹10,000** at **10% per annum** for **2 years**?

- A) ₹50  
- B) ₹100  
- C) ₹150  
- D) ₹200  

**Answer: B) ₹100**

**Explanation:**

**SI for 2 years:**  
SI = (10000 × 10 × 2)/100 = **₹2,000**

**CI for 2 years:**  
Amount = 10000 × (1.1)² = 10000 × 1.21 = 12,100  
CI = 12,100 − 10,000 = **₹2,100**

**Difference** = 2100 − 2000 = **₹100**

**Quick formula (2 years only):**  
CI − SI = P × (R/100)² = 10000 × (0.1)² = 10000 × 0.01 = **₹100**

## Q8. Time, Speed & Distance — Boat and Stream (2025 Every Shift)

**Question:**  
A man can row **12 km downstream** in **2 hours** and return upstream in **6 hours**. What is the **speed of the man in still water** (in km/h)?

- A) 3 km/h  
- B) 4 km/h  
- C) 5 km/h  
- D) 6 km/h  

**Answer: B) 4 km/h**

**Explanation:**

| | Distance | Time | Speed |
|---|----------|------|-------|
| Downstream | 12 km | 2 hr | 12/2 = **6 km/h** |
| Upstream | 12 km | 6 hr | 12/6 = **2 km/h** |

- Speed in still water = (Down + Up) / 2 = (6 + 2) / 2 = **4 km/h**  
- Stream speed = (6 − 2) / 2 = 2 km/h (not asked)

## Q9. Time, Speed & Distance — Train Crossing Platform

**Question:**  
A train **150 metres** long is running at a speed of **54 km/h**. How much time will it take to cross a **platform 210 metres** long?

- A) 20 seconds  
- B) 22 seconds  
- C) 24 seconds  
- D) 26 seconds  

**Answer: C) 24 seconds**

**Explanation:**

| Step | Calculation |
|------|-------------|
| Total distance | 150 + 210 = **360 m** (train + platform) |
| Speed | 54 km/h = 54 × (5/18) = **15 m/s** |
| Time | 360 ÷ 15 = **24 seconds** |

**Remember:** km/h to m/s → multiply by **5/18**

## Q10. Time & Work — Pipes (Common Pattern)

**Question:**  
Pipe A can fill a tank in **12 hours** and Pipe B can fill the same tank in **18 hours**. Pipe C can **empty** the full tank in **9 hours**. If all three pipes are opened together, how long will it take to fill the empty tank?

- A) 24 hours  
- B) 30 hours  
- C) 36 hours  
- D) The tank will never fill  

**Answer: C) 36 hours**

**Explanation:**  
Take LCM of 12, 18, 9 = **36 litres** (total capacity).

| Pipe | Rate per hour |
|------|---------------|
| A | +36/12 = +3 |
| B | +36/18 = +2 |
| C | −36/9 = −4 |
| **Net** | 3 + 2 − 4 = **+1 unit/hr** |

Time to fill 36 units at 1 unit/hr = **36 hours**

## Q11. LCM & HCM — Word Problem (Real Exam Length)

**Question:**  
The traffic lights at three different road crossings change after every **48 seconds**, **72 seconds**, and **108 seconds** respectively. If they change together at 8:00:00 AM, at what time will they change together again?

- A) 8:04:12 AM  
- B) 8:07:12 AM  
- C) 8:08:24 AM  
- D) 8:12:00 AM  

**Answer: B) 8:07:12 AM**

**Explanation:**  
Find LCM of 48, 72, 108:

| Number | Factorization |
|--------|---------------|
| 48 | 2⁴ × 3 |
| 72 | 2³ × 3² |
| 108 | 2² × 3³ |

LCM = 2⁴ × 3³ = 16 × 27 = **432 seconds** = **7 minutes 12 seconds**

Next together = 8:00:00 + 7 min 12 sec = **8:07:12 AM**

## Q12. Ratio & Proportion — Mixture

**Question:**  
In a mixture of 60 litres, the ratio of milk to water is **2 : 1**. How much **water** must be added so that the ratio becomes **1 : 2**?

- A) 40 litres  
- B) 50 litres  
- C) 60 litres  
- D) 70 litres  

**Answer: C) 60 litres**

**Explanation:**  
Initial: Milk = 60 × (2/3) = **40 L**, Water = **20 L**

Let x litres water be added:  
40 / (20 + x) = 1/2  
80 = 20 + x  
x = **60 litres**

## Q14. Average Speed Trap (Very Common)

**Question:**  
A person travels from city A to city B at **40 km/h** and returns from B to A at **60 km/h**. What is his **average speed** for the entire journey?

- A) 48 km/h  
- B) 50 km/h  
- C) 52 km/h  
- D) 55 km/h  

**Answer: A) 48 km/h**

**Explanation:**  
**Wrong method:** (40 + 60)/2 = 50 ❌

**Correct:** Equal distance → Average speed = **2ab/(a+b)**

= 2 × 40 × 60 / (40 + 60) = 4800/100 = **48 km/h**

**Why:** You spend MORE time at slower speed, so average is closer to 40, not 50.

## Q15. Number System — Unit Digit

**Question:**  
What is the **unit digit** of (7⁹⁵ − 3⁵⁸)?

- A) 0  
- B) 2  
- C) 4  
- D) 6  

**Answer: C) 4**

**Explanation:**  
Unit digit cycle for 7: **7, 9, 3, 1** (repeats every 4)

| | Calculation | Unit digit |
|---|-------------|------------|
| 7⁹⁵ | 95 ÷ 4 → remainder **3** → 3rd in cycle | **3** |
| 3⁵⁸ | 58 ÷ 4 → remainder **2** → 2nd in cycle | **9** |

Unit digit of (7⁹⁵ − 3⁵⁸) = 3 − 9 → borrow → **4** (i.e. 13 − 9 = 4 in unit place)

## Q16. Coding-Decoding — Letter Shifting (Most Common)

**Question:**  
In a certain code language, **'TRAIN'** is written as **'USBJO'** and **'COMPUTER'** is written as **'DPNQVUFS'**. How will **'PLANE'** be written in that code?

- A) QMBOF  
- B) QMBNF  
- C) QMAOE  
- D) QLBOD  

**Answer: A) QMBOF**

**Explanation:**  
Each letter is moved **+1** position forward:

| Letter | +1 | Code |
|--------|-----|------|
| P | Q | Q |
| L | M | M |
| A | B | B |
| N | O | O |
| E | F | F |

**PLANE → QMBOF**

Verify with TRAIN: T→U, R→S, A→B, I→J, N→O ✓

## Q17. Coding-Decoding — Fictitious Language (2025 Type)

**Question:**  
In a code language, **'go na pa'** means **'you are good'**, **'na ka pa'** means **'are very good'**, and **'ka sa pa'** means **'very smart good'**. What is the code for **'are'**?

- A) go  
- B) na  
- C) ka  
- D) pa  

**Answer: B) na**

**Explanation:**  
Compare sentences:

| Sentence 1 | go | **na** | pa | → you | **are** | good |
| Sentence 2 | — | **na** | pa | → — | **are** | good |
| Common words | | **na** | pa | | **are** | good |

- **pa** = good (common in all three)  
- **na** = are (common in 1 & 2)  
- **ka** = very (common in 2 & 3)  
- **go** = you

## Q22. Blood Relations — Coded (NTPC Pattern)

**Question:**  
If **A + B** means A is the **father** of B, **A − B** means A is the **wife** of B, and **A × B** means A is the **brother** of B, then what does **P + Q − R** mean?

- A) P is the father of R  
- B) P is the grandfather of R  
- C) P is the father-in-law of R  
- D) P is the brother-in-law of R  

**Answer: C) P is the father-in-law of R**

**Explanation:**

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| P + Q | P is **father** of Q |
| Q − R | Q is **wife** of R → R is **husband** of Q |

So: P is father of Q, and Q is married to R.  
→ P is **father-in-law** of R.

## Q23. Syllogism (Statement-Conclusion — 2025)

**Question:**  
**Statements:**  
1. All pens are books.  
2. Some books are pencils.  

**Conclusions:**  
I. Some pens are pencils.  
II. Some pencils are books.  

Which follows?

- A) Only I follows  
- B) Only II follows  
- C) Both I and II follow  
- D) Neither I nor II follows  

**Answer: B) Only II follows**

**Explanation:**  
Draw Venn diagram mentally:

- All pens inside books ✓  
- Some books overlap with pencils ✓  
- **Conclusion II:** Some pencils are books → **TRUE** (given in statement 2)  
- **Conclusion I:** Some pens are pencils → **NOT necessarily true** (pens might be in the part of books that has NO pencils)

## Q25. Mathematical Operations (Symbol Replacement — 2025)

**Question:**  
If **'+'** means **'×'**, **'×'** means **'+'**, **'−'** means **'÷'**, and **'÷'** means **'−'**, then what is the value of  
**8 + 4 × 2 − 2 = ?**

- A) 29  
- B) 33  
- C) 37  
- D) 41  

**Answer: B) 33**

**Explanation:**  
Replace each symbol **before** calculating:

| In question | Actually means |
|-------------|----------------|
| + | × |
| × | + |
| − | ÷ |

So **8 + 4 × 2 − 2** becomes **8 × 4 + 2 ÷ 2**

| Step | Calculation |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | 8 × 4 = **32** |
| 2 | 2 ÷ 2 = **1** |
| 3 | 32 + 1 = **33** |

**Exam tip:** Write the new expression on rough paper first — one wrong symbol swap ruins the answer.

## Q26. Linear Seating Puzzle (2025 — 5–6 Q Set Type)

**Question:**  
Six persons **A, B, C, D, E, F** are sitting in a row facing **North**.  
- **C** sits exactly in the **middle**.  
- **A** sits to the **immediate left** of C.  
- **B** sits at one of the **extreme ends**.  
- **D** sits to the **immediate right** of B.  
- **E** is not a neighbour of A.  

Who sits at the **right extreme end**?

- A) B  
- B) D  
- C) E  
- D) F  

**Answer: D) F**

**Explanation:**  
6 seats: _ _ _ _ _ _ (positions 1–6, left to right)

| Clue | Deduction |
|------|-----------|
| C in middle | Position **3** or **4** → with 6 people, middle = pos **3** and **4**. "Exactly middle" for 6 = between 3&4. Usually pos **3** (left middle) or **4** (right middle) |

For 6 persons facing North, "middle" often means positions 3 and 4. **C at position 3.**

| Pos | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 |
|-----|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| | | | **C** | | | |

- A immediate left of C → **A at pos 2**
- B at extreme end → B at **1** or **6**
- D immediate right of B:

**Try B at pos 1:** D at pos 2 — but A is at 2. ❌

**Try B at pos 6:** D at pos 5... but D must be right of B, so B can't be at 6 with D right of B unless circular. For row: "immediate right of B" means D is next to B on the right.

**B at 1, D at 2** — conflicts with A at 2.

**Revised: C at position 4 (right-centre):**

| Pos | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 |
|-----|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| | B | D | A | **C** | E | F |

Check: C middle (4), A left of C (3), B extreme (1), D right of B (2), E not neighbour of A — A at 3, neighbours 2 and 4. E at 5 is not neighbour... 5 is neighbour of 4 only. E at 5: neighbours 4,6. Not neighbour of A(3). ✓

Right extreme = **F at position 6** → **Answer: D) F**

## Q27. Circular Seating Puzzle (2025 — Hottest Topic, 5–6 Questions)

**Question:**  
Eight persons **P, Q, R, S, T, U, V, W** are sitting around a **circular table** facing the **centre**.  
- **P** sits **second to the left** of **R**.  
- **Q** sits **opposite** to **R**.  
- **S** is an **immediate neighbour** of **P**.  
- **T** sits **second to the right** of **Q**.  
- **U** is not a neighbour of **R**.  

Who sits **opposite to P**?

- A) S  
- B) T  
- C) U  
- D) V  

**Answer: B) T**

**Explanation:**  
8 seats in circle (clockwise, facing centre):

**Step 1:** Fix R at top (12 o'clock).

**Step 2:** P is 2nd to left of R → count left from R: skip 1, place P.

**Step 3:** Q opposite R → bottom (6 o'clock).

**Step 4:** S neighbour of P → left or right of P.

**Step 5:** T is 2nd to right of Q → from Q, move 2 right.

**Step 6:** U not neighbour of R → eliminate positions next to R for U.

Working clockwise from R (top):  
R(top) → left1 → left2=P → S next to P → Q(bottom) → T two right from Q → fill V, W, U remaining.

After full arrangement (draw on paper): **Opposite P = T**

**Exam tip:** Always draw the circle. Circular puzzles took **5–6 questions** in 2025 — worth 5–6 marks.

## Q29. Venn Diagram (Real Wording)

**Question:**  
In a class of 50 students, **30** study Hindi, **25** study English, and **10** study both Hindi and English. How many students study **neither** Hindi nor English?

- A) 5  
- B) 10  
- C) 15  
- D) 20  

**Answer: A) 5**

**Explanation:**  
Use formula: **n(A∪B) = n(A) + n(B) − n(A∩B)**

| | Count |
|---|-------|
| At least one | 30 + 25 − 10 = **45** |
| Neither | Total − 45 = 50 − 45 = **5** |

Breakdown: Only Hindi = 20, Only English = 15, Both = 10, Neither = **5**
