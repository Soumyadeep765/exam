# RRB NTPC 2023 — Previous Year Paper Analysis

> **Exam Cycle:** CEN 03/2023 (Undergraduate Level) + ongoing CEN 01/2019 stages  
> **Stage:** CBT 1 (new recruitment) + residual CBT 2/CBAT stages  
> **Sources:** RRB official notifications, Testbook PYQ analysis, Career Power syllabus weightage  
> **Last Updated:** June 2026

---

## 1. Exam Overview

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Notification | CEN 03/2023 (UG Level posts) |
| Total Questions (CBT 1) | 100 |
| Duration | 90 minutes |
| Sections | GA (40) + Math (30) + Reasoning (30) |
| Overall Difficulty | **Moderate** |
| Key Feature | Increased focus on application-based science |

**Key Insight:** 2023 marked a **stabilization period** after the 2021 difficulty spike. Papers were moderate with balanced sections. Application-based General Science replaced rote NCERT fact recall in many shifts.

## 3. Mathematics Analysis (30 Questions)

### Topic Distribution

| Topic | Questions (Range) | Difficulty | Trend vs 2021 |
|-------|-------------------|------------|---------------|
| Simplification & BODMAS | 3–4 | Easy | Stable |
| Number System | 3–4 | Easy–Moderate | Stable |
| Percentage | 2–3 | Moderate | ↓ slightly easier |
| Ratio & Proportion | 2–3 | Moderate | Stable |
| Profit & Loss & Discount | 3–4 | Moderate | ↑ increased |
| SI & CI | 2–3 | Moderate | Stable |
| Time & Work | 1–2 | Moderate | ↓ fewer questions |
| Time, Speed & Distance | 2–3 | Moderate | Stable |
| Mensuration | 2–3 | Moderate | Stable |
| LCM & HCF | 2–3 | Easy | Stable |
| Algebra | 1–2 | Easy–Moderate | Stable |
| Geometry & Trigonometry | 1–2 | Moderate | Stable |
| Data Interpretation | 1–2 | Moderate | ↑ emerging |
| Statistics | 1 | Easy | Stable |
| Pipe & Cistern | 1 | Moderate | Occasional |

### Difficulty Profile
- **50% Easy-to-Moderate**, 50% Moderate — more balanced than 2021
- Profit & Loss had **successive discount** and **dishonest dealer** types
- DI questions used simple tables (2–3 rows)

### Repeated Question Patterns
1. Successive discount (10% + 10% ≠ 20%)
2. Percentage increase/decrease in population
3. Average speed (upstream + downstream)
4. Work efficiency with different workers
5. Simple DI: find percentage of total

### Difficulty Profile
- Reasoning was the **easiest section** in most 2023 shifts
- Coding-Decoding dominated with letter-number mixed codes
- Puzzle complexity reduced compared to 2021–2022

### Repeated Question Patterns
1. Reverse letter coding (Z=A, Y=B pattern)
2. Missing term in number series (difference-based)
3. Circular seating arrangement (8 persons)
4. Syllogism with Venn diagram verification
5. Clock angle problems (occasional)

## 8. Emerging Trends (2023)

1. **Application-based science** — "Why does X happen?" instead of "What is X?"
2. **Computer literacy questions** increased (3–4 per shift)
3. **DI questions** appearing in CBT 1 (previously CBT 2 only)
4. **Space program GK** — ISRO missions heavily featured
5. **Environment Act/schemes** — 1–2 questions per shift

---

## 9. Preparation Takeaways

1. **2023 was a moderate year** — good for building confidence
2. **Science: understand concepts**, not just memorize facts
3. **G20 + ISRO missions** are high-probability CA topics
4. **Computer basics** now a fixed 3–4 question slot
5. **Profit & Loss** regained prominence — practice all variants
