### Difficulty Profile
- **Speed-Time-Distance** and **Profit & Loss** were the dominant arithmetic topics
- DI questions used bar graphs and pie charts
- 2-step calculation minimum for most moderate questions

### Repeated Question Patterns
1. Train crossing platform/pole (TSD)
2. Marked price → discount → profit% chain
3. CI amount after 2 years, find rate
4. LCM-HCF product relationship (a×b = LCM×HCF)
5. Bar graph DI with year-on-year comparison

| Direction & Distance | 1–2 | Easy |
| Statement-Conclusion | 1–2 | Moderate |
| Jumbling (Word Rearrangement) | 1 | Easy |
| Non-Verbal | 1 | Easy |

### Difficulty Profile
- **Puzzle-based questions increased** to 3–4 per shift
- Floor-based puzzles appeared (new pattern)
- Analogies decreased slightly vs 2016–2019 era

### Repeated Question Patterns
1. Floor puzzle: 5 floors, 5 persons, attributes
2. Fictitious language coding-decoding
3. Alphabet series with skip pattern (A, C, F, J...)
4. Venn: Students who play cricket/football/both
5. Syllogism with "some" and "all" mixed

## 9. Preparation Takeaways

1. **Computers are now mandatory** — dedicate 2–3 days to MS Office + abbreviations
2. **Elections + Olympics years** = heavy CA weightage
3. **Floor-based puzzles** — new pattern to practice
4. **TSD + Profit/Loss** remain the highest-yield math topics
5. **New criminal laws (BNS/BNSS)** — high-probability polity topic
