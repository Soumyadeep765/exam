# RRB NTPC 2021 — Previous Year Paper Analysis

> **Exam Cycle:** CEN 01/2019 | **Stage:** CBT 1 (Stage 1)  
> **Exam Dates:** 28 December 2020 – 13 January 2021 (multiple shifts)  
> **Sources:** Official RRB syllabus (CEN 01/2019), shift-wise memory-based analysis, PrepGrind difficulty study  
> **Last Updated:** June 2026

---

## 1. Exam Overview

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Total Questions | 100 |
| Duration | 90 minutes |
| Negative Marking | 1/3 mark per wrong answer |
| Sections | GA (40) + Mathematics (30) + Reasoning (30) |
| Overall Difficulty | **Tough** (historically one of the hardest NTPC cycles) |
| Approx. Cutoff (General) | ~67 marks (varied by RRB zone) |

**Key Insight:** 2021 CBT 1 is widely regarded as the **toughest NTPC cycle** since 2016. Mathematics was calculation-heavy with multi-step problems. Reasoning introduced trickier patterns. This cycle set the benchmark for post-2019 difficulty increase.

## 3. Mathematics Analysis (30 Questions)

### Topic Distribution

| Topic | Questions (Range) | Difficulty | Priority |
|-------|-------------------|------------|----------|
| Arithmetic (Combined) | 18–22 | Tough | ★★★★★ |
| Number System (LCM/HCF, Divisibility) | 4–5 | Moderate–Tough | ★★★★★ |
| Percentage | 2–3 | Tough | ★★★★★ |
| Ratio & Proportion | 2–3 | Tough | ★★★★ |
| Profit & Loss | 2–3 | Moderate–Tough | ★★★★ |
| Time & Work | 2–3 | **Very Tough** | ★★★★★ |
| Time, Speed & Distance | 2–3 | Tough | ★★★★ |
| Simple & Compound Interest | 2–3 | Moderate | ★★★★ |
| Mensuration | 2–3 | Moderate | ★★★ |
| Simplification/BODMAS | 3–4 | Moderate | ★★★★ |
| Algebra | 1–2 | Moderate | ★★★ |
| Geometry & Trigonometry | 1–2 | Moderate | ★★★ |
| Data Interpretation | 0–1 | — | ★★ |
| Statistics (Mean/Median/Mode) | 1 | Easy | ★★★ |

### Difficulty Profile
- **70% Moderate-to-Tough** arithmetic questions
- Multi-step calculations common (2–3 operations per question)
- Time & Work and Ratio problems required advanced techniques
- Less direct formula application; more conceptual traps

### Repeated Question Patterns
1. **Successive percentage change** (increase then decrease)
2. **Pipe & Cistern** combined with Time & Work
3. **Boat & Stream** TSD problems
4. **Partnership** profit sharing
5. **LCM-HCF** application in word problems

## 4. Reasoning Analysis (30 Questions)

### Topic Distribution

| Topic | Questions (Range) | Difficulty | Priority |
|-------|-------------------|------------|----------|
| Coding-Decoding | 4–5 | Moderate | ★★★★★ |
| Number & Alphabet Series | 4–5 | Moderate–Tough | ★★★★★ |
| Analogy & Classification | 4–5 | Moderate | ★★★★ |
| Syllogism | 2–3 | Tough | ★★★★ |
| Blood Relations | 2–3 | Moderate | ★★★★ |
| Mathematical Operations | 2–3 | Moderate | ★★★ |
| Puzzles (Linear/Circular) | 2–3 | Tough | ★★★★ |
| Venn Diagrams | 2 | Easy–Moderate | ★★★ |
| Direction Sense | 1–2 | Easy | ★★★ |
| Statement-Conclusion | 1–2 | Moderate | ★★★ |
| Non-Verbal (Mirror/Figure) | 1–2 | Easy | ★★ |

### Difficulty Profile
- Syllogism questions used **only-conclusion** and **either-or** type traps
- Series included **mixed alphanumeric** patterns (new for many aspirants)
- Puzzle seating arrangements were 4–5 variable complexity

### Repeated Question Patterns
1. Letter shifting coding (e.g., +2, -1 pattern)
2. Missing number in matrix/grid
3. Family tree with 3+ generation depth
4. Alphabet series with vowel-consonant alternation

## 8. Preparation Takeaways

1. **2021 set the difficulty bar** — post-2021 papers follow similar toughness
2. **Arithmetic mastery is non-negotiable** — 60%+ of math is arithmetic
3. **Science from NCERT** gives 10–12 free marks in GA
4. **Practice calculation speed** — 2021 math was time-consuming
5. **Don't skip puzzles** — became standard from 2021 onward
