## 1. Theory

**Percentage** means "per hundred." It expresses a number as a fraction of 100.

- Percentage = (Value / Total) × 100
- Value = (Percentage × Total) / 100
- Fraction to Percentage: multiply by 100
- Percentage to Fraction: divide by 100

### Key Concepts
- **Base:** The number on which percentage is calculated
- **Successive Change:** When multiple percentage changes apply one after another
- **Percentage Point:** Absolute difference between two percentages (NOT same as percentage change)

---

## 2. Formulas

| Concept | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| Basic Percentage | x% of N = (x/100) × N |
| Percentage Change | ((New − Old) / Old) × 100 |
| Successive Change | Net = a + b + (ab/100) for +a% then +b% |
| Successive Decrease | Net = −a − b + (ab/100) for −a% then −b% |
| Increase then Decrease | +a% then −a% ≠ 0 (always a net loss) |
| Population Growth | Final = Initial × (1 + r/100)ⁿ |
| Depreciation | Final = Initial × (1 − r/100)ⁿ |
| Fraction equivalents | 1/2=50%, 1/3≈33.33%, 1/4=25%, 1/5=20%, 1/8=12.5%, 1/10=10% |

### Successive Change Shortcut
If a value increases by a% and then by b%:
```
Net change = a + b + (a×b)/100 %
```

## 3. Examples

### Example 1: Basic Percentage
**Q:** What is 35% of 480?  
**Solution:** (35/100) × 480 = 168

### Example 2: Percentage Change
**Q:** Price increased from ₹200 to ₹250. Find percentage increase.  
**Solution:** ((250−200)/200) × 100 = 25%

### Example 3: Successive Change
**Q:** Population of 50,000 increases by 10% and then by 20%. Find final population.  
**Solution:**  
Net change = 10 + 20 + (10×20)/100 = 32%  
Final = 50,000 × 1.32 = **66,000**

### Example 4: Increase then Decrease Trap
**Q:** Price increased by 20% and then decreased by 20%. Net change?  
**Solution:**  
Net = 20 − 20 + (20×(−20))/100 = −4%  
**Always a loss of 4%** (not zero!)

### Example 5: Election/Vote Problem
**Q:** A candidate gets 45% votes and wins by 12000 votes. Total votes?  
**Solution:**  
Margin = 45% − 55%... Winner 45%, loser 55%? No — winner gets MORE.  
If winner gets 55% and loser 45%:  
Difference = 10% of total = 12000  
Total = 12000/0.10 = **1,20,000**

## 4. Shortcuts

| Shortcut | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| **10% rule** | Move decimal one place left for 10% |
| **1% rule** | Move decimal two places left for 1% |
| **5% = half of 10%** | Quick mental calculation |
| **25% = 1/4** | Divide by 4 directly |
| **12.5% = 1/8** | Divide by 8 directly |
| **Successive formula** | a + b + ab/100 — memorize this |
| **Comparing fractions** | Convert to percentages for quick comparison |

## 5. Previous Year Questions (Pattern-Based)

**PYQ Type 1 (NTPC 2021):** A number is increased by 25% and then decreased by 25%. Find net percentage change.  
**Answer:** −6.25% (loss)

**PYQ Type 2 (NTPC 2023):** If 40% of a number is 120, find the number.  
**Answer:** 120 × 100/40 = 300

**PYQ Type 3 (NTPC 2025):** Population of town is 80,000. It increases by 15% in first year and 10% in second year. Find population after 2 years.  
**Solution:** Net = 15 + 10 + 1.5 = 26.5%. Final = 80,000 × 1.265 = 1,01,200

**PYQ Type 4 (NTPC 2024):** In an exam, a student scores 30% and fails by 15 marks. Passing marks are 45%. Find maximum marks.  
**Solution:** Difference = 45% − 30% = 15% = 15 marks. Total = 15/0.15 = 100

## 6. Practice MCQs

**Q1.** 20% of 450 = ?  
A) 80  B) 90  C) 100  D) 110  
**Answer: B** (90)

**Q2.** A shirt marked at ₹800 is sold at 15% discount. SP = ?  
A) ₹680  B) ₹690  C) ₹700  D) ₹720  
**Answer: A** (800 × 0.85 = 680)

**Q3.** Price increased by 10% then decreased by 10%. Net change?  
A) 0%  B) −1%  C) +1%  D) −2%  
**Answer: B** (10 − 10 − 1 = −1%)

**Q4.** 60% of (x + 20) = 48. Find x.  
A) 50  B) 60  C) 70  D) 80  
**Answer: B** (x + 20 = 80, x = 60)

**Q5.** If A is 25% more than B, then B is what % less than A?  
A) 20%  B) 25%  C) 30%  D) 15%  
**Answer: A** (25/125 × 100 = 20%)

**Q6.** Population 2,00,000. Increases 20% then decreases 10%. Final population?  
A) 2,12,000  B) 2,16,000  C) 2,20,000  D) 2,24,000  
**Answer: B** (Net = 20 − 10 − 2 = 8%. 2,00,000 × 1.08 = 2,16,000)

**Q7.** 33⅓% of 90 = ?  
A) 25  B) 30  C) 35  D) 40  
**Answer: B** (1/3 × 90 = 30)

**Q8.** A number is first increased by 20% and then decreased by 20%. If final value is 480, find original.  
A) 480  B) 500  C) 520  D) 540  
**Answer: B** (Net = −4%. Original = 480/0.96 = 500)

**Q9.** In an election, candidate A got 55% votes and won by 20,000 votes. Total votes?  
A) 1,00,000  B) 1,50,000  C) 2,00,000  D) 2,50,000  
**Answer: C** (10% = 20,000. Total = 2,00,000)

**Q10.** Express 0.075 as percentage.  
A) 7.5%  B) 0.75%  C) 75%  D) 0.075%  
**Answer: A**

## 7. Common Mistakes

1. Confusing **percentage point** with **percentage change**
2. Adding successive percentages directly (20% + 20% ≠ 40%)
3. Taking wrong base for percentage calculation
4. Forgetting that +a% then −a% always results in net loss

---

## 8. Exam Strategy

- Target time: **45–60 seconds** per question
- Always identify the **base value** first
- For successive changes, use the formula — don't guess
- Convert common fractions to percentages for speed
