# Tutorial: Simple & Compound Interest

> **Subject:** Mathematics | **NTPC Weightage:** 2–3 questions per CBT 1  
> **Difficulty:** Moderate | **Priority:** ★★★★  
> **Last Updated:** June 2026

---

## 1. Theory

**Simple Interest (SI):** Interest calculated only on principal.  
**Compound Interest (CI):** Interest calculated on principal + accumulated interest.

---

## 2. Formulas

| Concept | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| SI | (P × R × T) / 100 |
| Amount (SI) | P + SI = P(1 + RT/100) |
| CI (annual) | P(1 + R/100)ᵀ − P |
| Amount (CI) | P(1 + R/100)ᵀ |
| CI − SI (2 years) | P × (R/100)² |
| CI − SI (3 years) | P × (R/100) × (3 + R/100) |
| Half-yearly CI | Rate = R/2, Time = 2T |
| Quarterly CI | Rate = R/4, Time = 4T |
| Depreciation | Same as CI but subtracted |
| Installments | Use present value concept |

---

## 3. Examples

**Q:** SI on ₹5000 at 10% for 3 years?  
**A:** SI = 5000×10×3/100 = **₹1500**

**Q:** CI on ₹8000 at 10% for 2 years?  
**A:** Amount = 8000×1.1² = 9680. CI = **₹1680**

**Q:** Difference CI − SI for 2 years at 10% on ₹10000?  
**A:** 10000×(10/100)² = **₹100**

## 4. Practice MCQs

**Q1.** SI on ₹4000 at 5% for 4 years?  
A) ₹600  B) ₹700  C) ₹800  D) ₹900  
**Answer: C**

**Q2.** At what rate will ₹600 yield ₹180 SI in 3 years?  
A) 8%  B) 10%  C) 12%  D) 15%  
**Answer: B** (180=600×R×3/100 → R=10)

**Q3.** CI on ₹10000 at 20% for 2 years?  
A) ₹4000  B) ₹4400  C) ₹4200  D) ₹4600  
**Answer: B** (10000×1.44−10000=4400)

**Q4.** CI − SI for 2 years at 5% on ₹8000?  
A) ₹15  B) ₹20  C) ₹25  D) ₹30  
**Answer: B** (8000×0.0025=20)

**Q5.** Amount doubles in 5 years at SI. Rate?  
A) 15%  B) 20%  C) 25%  D) 10%  
**Answer: B** (P=SI in 5 yr → R=20%)

**Q6.** ₹15625 at 4% CI for 3 years. Amount?  
A) ₹17576  B) ₹17600  C) ₹18000  D) ₹18500  
**Answer: A** (15625×1.04³=17576)

**Q7.** Sum amounts to ₹980 in 2 yr SI at 7%. Sum?  
A) ₹850  B) ₹857.14  C) ₹860  D) ₹900  
**Answer: B**

**Q8.** CI half-yearly at 10% p.a. on ₹16000 for 1 year?  
A) ₹1600  B) ₹1680  C) ₹1681  D) ₹1700  
**Answer: C** (16000×1.05⁴−16000)

**Q9.** In how many years will ₹5000 at 12% SI become ₹8000?  
A) 4  B) 5  C) 6  D) 7  
**Answer: B** (SI=3000, 3000=5000×12×T/100 → T=5)

**Q10.** Rate at which CI and SI equal for 1 year?  
A) Any rate  B) 0%  C) 10%  D) Never equal for T>1  
**Answer: A** (For T=1, CI=SI always)

## 5. Exam Strategy

- **CI − SI difference (2 years)** = P(R/100)² — fast formula
- Amount doubles at R% in 72/R years (approx rule)
- NTPC asks mostly 2-year CI and SI difference
