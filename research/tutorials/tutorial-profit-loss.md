# Tutorial: Profit and Loss

> **Subject:** Mathematics | **NTPC Weightage:** 3–4 questions per CBT 1  
> **Difficulty:** Moderate | **Priority:** ★★★★★  
> **Last Updated:** June 2026

---

## 1. Theory

**Profit and Loss** deals with the financial outcome of buying and selling goods.

- **Cost Price (CP):** Price at which an article is purchased
- **Selling Price (SP):** Price at which an article is sold
- **Marked Price (MP):** Price tagged on the article (before discount)
- **Profit:** SP > CP → Profit = SP − CP
- **Loss:** SP < CP → Loss = CP − SP
- **Discount:** Reduction on Marked Price → Discount = MP − SP

---

## 2. Formulas

| Concept | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| Profit | SP − CP |
| Loss | CP − SP |
| Profit % | (Profit / CP) × 100 |
| Loss % | (Loss / CP) × 100 |
| SP (with profit) | CP × (1 + P/100) |
| SP (with loss) | CP × (1 − L/100) |
| CP from SP (profit) | SP × 100 / (100 + P) |
| CP from SP (loss) | SP × 100 / (100 − L) |
| Discount | MP − SP |
| Discount % | (Discount / MP) × 100 |
| SP after discount | MP × (1 − D/100) |
| Successive discounts | Net = D₁ + D₂ − (D₁×D₂)/100 |
| Dishonest dealer | Uses false weight → Effective CP changes |

### Dishonest Dealer Formula
If dealer claims to sell at CP but uses weight x instead of y:
```
Gain% = ((y − x) / x) × 100
```

### Profit = Loss Condition
If SP of x articles = CP of y articles:
```
Profit% = ((y − x) / x) × 100
```

## 3. Examples

### Example 1: Basic Profit
**Q:** CP = ₹400, SP = ₹500. Find profit percentage.  
**Solution:** Profit = 100. Profit% = (100/400) × 100 = **25%**

### Example 2: Marked Price & Discount
**Q:** MP = ₹1000, discount = 20%. Find SP.  
**Solution:** SP = 1000 × 0.80 = **₹800**

### Example 3: Successive Discount
**Q:** MP = ₹2000. Two successive discounts of 10% and 15%. Find SP.  
**Solution:**  
Net discount = 10 + 15 − (10×15)/100 = 23.5%  
SP = 2000 × 0.765 = **₹1530**

### Example 4: Dishonest Dealer
**Q:** Dealer sells at CP but uses 900g weight for 1kg. Find profit%.  
**Solution:** Gain% = ((1000−900)/900) × 100 = **11.11%**

### Example 5: CP/SP Chain (NTPC Pattern)
**Q:** A man sells two articles at ₹99 each. On one he gains 10%, on other loses 10%. Net result?  
**Solution:**  
Always a **net loss** when profit% = loss% on equal SP.  
Loss% = (10)²/100 = **1% loss**

## 4. Shortcuts

| Shortcut | When to Use |
|----------|-------------|
| SP = CP × (100±P)/100 | Direct profit/loss % given |
| CP = SP × 100/(100±P) | Find CP from SP |
| Successive discount: a+b−ab/100 | Two discounts on MP |
| Equal SP, equal % profit & loss → net loss = P²/100 | Classic trap question |
| MP = CP × (100+Profit%)/(100−Discount%) | Find MP from CP |
| 100/100 rule | CP = 100, calculate SP directly |

## 5. Previous Year Questions (Pattern-Based)

**PYQ Type 1 (NTPC 2025):** A shopkeeper marks his goods 40% above CP and gives 20% discount. Profit%?  
**Solution:** SP = 140 × 0.80 = 112. Profit% = 12%

**PYQ Type 2 (NTPC 2024):** Selling 12 pens at SP of 10 pens = 20% profit. Find profit%.  
**Solution:** Profit% = ((12−10)/10) × 100 = 20%

**PYQ Type 3 (NTPC 2023):** CP of 20 articles = SP of 25 articles. Find profit or loss%.  
**Solution:** Loss% = ((25−20)/25) × 100 = 20% loss

**PYQ Type 4 (NTPC 2022):** After 10% discount, SP = ₹450. Find MP.  
**Solution:** MP = 450/0.90 = ₹500

## 6. Practice MCQs

**Q1.** CP = ₹250, SP = ₹300. Profit% = ?  
A) 15%  B) 20%  C) 25%  D) 30%  
**Answer: B**

**Q2.** MP = ₹800, discount = 25%. SP = ?  
A) ₹600  B) ₹550  C) ₹650  D) ₹700  
**Answer: A**

**Q3.** A sells at 20% profit. If CP = ₹500, SP = ?  
A) ₹550  B) ₹600  C) ₹650  D) ₹700  
**Answer: B**

**Q4.** Successive discounts 20% and 10% = net discount of:  
A) 30%  B) 28%  C) 25%  D) 32%  
**Answer: B** (20+10−2=28%)

**Q5.** Dishonest dealer uses 850g for 1kg. Profit% = ?  
A) 15%  B) 17.65%  C) 20%  D) 12.5%  
**Answer: B** ((1000−850)/850 × 100 = 17.65%)

**Q6.** SP of 15 items = CP of 18 items. Profit% = ?  
A) 15%  B) 20%  C) 25%  D) 18%  
**Answer: B** ((18−15)/15 × 100 = 20%)

**Q7.** Article sold at 10% loss for ₹270. CP = ?  
A) ₹280  B) ₹300  C) ₹320  D) ₹350  
**Answer: B** (270/0.90 = 300)

**Q8.** Marked 50% above CP, discount 20%. Profit% = ?  
A) 15%  B) 20%  C) 25%  D) 30%  
**Answer: B** (SP = 150×0.80 = 120. Profit = 20%)

**Q9.** Two articles sold at ₹1200 each — 20% profit on one, 20% loss on other. Overall?  
A) 4% profit  B) 4% loss  C) No profit no loss  D) 2% loss  
**Answer: B** (Loss = 20²/100 = 4%)

**Q10.** If SP is doubled, profit becomes triple. Find original profit%.  
A) 50%  B) 66.67%  C) 100%  D) 75%  
**Answer: C** (Let CP=100, profit=P. 2×SP = 100+3P. SP=100+P. So 2(100+P)=100+3P → P=100)

---

## 7. Common Mistakes

1. Calculating profit% on SP instead of CP
2. Adding successive discounts (10%+20% ≠ 30%)
3. Forgetting dishonest dealer uses false weight on CP side
4. Equal profit% and loss% on same SP → always net loss

---

## 8. Exam Strategy

- **Most asked NTPC math topic** after simplification
- Always calculate on **CP base** for profit/loss %
- Draw CP = 100 for quick percentage calculations
- Watch for MP → Discount → SP chains (multi-step)
