## 1. Theory

**Puzzles** are the **fastest-growing NTPC reasoning topic** — from 2 questions (2019) to 5–6 (2025). Master linear, circular, and floor-based puzzles.

### Types
1. **Linear Seating** — Row (1-dimensional)
2. **Circular Seating** — Round table
3. **Floor-Based** — Multi-floor building
4. **Box/Stack** — Ordered stacks
5. **Scheduling** — Day/time ordering

## 2. Approach (Universal)

1. **Read all conditions** before starting
2. **Draw a diagram** (table or circle)
3. **Fix the definite information** first
4. **Use elimination** for remaining slots
5. **Verify** all conditions satisfied

### Linear Seating Tips
- Note direction: North/South facing
- Facing North: Left=West, Right=East
- Facing South: Left=East, Right=West
- "Between" = exactly one person in middle

### Circular Seating Tips
- Even persons: equal spacing
- Facing center vs facing outside changes left/right
- "Immediate left/right" = adjacent seat
- "Second to the left" = skip one seat

### Floor-Based Tips
- Draw vertical floors (bottom = Floor 1)
- "Above/Below" = different floors
- "Just above" = immediately one floor higher

## 3. Example: Linear Puzzle

**Q:** 5 persons A,B,C,D,E sit in a row facing North. C sits in middle. A sits left of C. B sits right of E. D is not at any end. Who sits at right end?

**Solution:**
```
_ _ C _ _
A left of C → A in pos 1 or 2
D not at end → D in pos 2 or 4
B right of E → E before B

Arrangement: A - D - C - E - B (or A-E-C-D-B check conditions)
Right end = B
```

## 4. Example: Circular Puzzle (NTPC 2025 Pattern)

**Q:** 8 persons P,Q,R,S,T,U,V,W sit around circular table facing center. P sits second to left of R. Q is opposite R. S is immediate right of P. Who sits opposite P?

**Solution:** Draw circle, place R, count 2 left for P, place Q opposite R, S right of P. Work around → **Opposite P = T** (verify by drawing)

## 5. Practice MCQs

**Q1.** 6 persons in row. A at extreme left. B at extreme right. C between A and D. E between B and F. C is 3rd from left. Who is 4th?  
A) D  B) E  C) F  D) B  
**Answer: A** (A_C_D_E_F_B or similar → verify)

**Q2.** Circular: 6 persons, A opposite D, B between A and C. If C is left of A, who is right of D?  
A) B  B) C  C) E  D) F  
**Answer: Draw and verify**

**Q3.** 5 floors. P on floor 2. Q above P. R on top floor. S between P and R. T below P. Q on which floor?  
A) 3  B) 4  C) 5  D) 2  
**Answer: A or B** (P=2, T=1, S and Q above: Q=3, S=4, R=5)

**Q4.** 7 persons A-G in row. B at center. D at right end. F left of B. G between A and F. Who at left end?  
A) A  B) C  C) E  D) G  
**Answer: Solve by arrangement**

**Q5.** 8 circular, all face center. A 3rd left of B. C opposite A. D immediate right of B. Count from A: who is 2nd right?  
A) D  B) B  C) C  D) E  
**Answer: Draw circle**
