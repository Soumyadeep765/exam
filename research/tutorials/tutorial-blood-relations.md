# Tutorial: Blood Relations

> **Subject:** Reasoning | **NTPC Weightage:** 2–3 questions per CBT 1  
> **Difficulty:** Easy–Moderate | **Priority:** ★★★★  
> **Last Updated:** June 2026

---

## 1. Theory

**Blood Relations** tests family tree understanding. Draw diagrams — never solve mentally for complex problems.

### Key Terms
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Paternal | Father's side |
| Maternal | Mother's side |
| Sibling | Brother or Sister |
| Cousin | Child of parent's sibling |
| Nephew/Niece | Sibling's child |
| Uncle/Aunt | Parent's sibling |
| Grand | One generation up (Grandfather) |
| In-law | Through marriage |

---

## 2. Approach

1. **Draw a family tree** with symbols (+ male, − female)
2. Start from the **given person**
3. Work **one relation at a time**
4. For coded relations, decode step by step

---

## 3. Examples

**Q:** A is B's father. B is C's sister. How is A related to C?  
**Solution:** A → father → B → sister → C. A is **father** of C.

**Q:** Pointing to a man, Ravi says "He is the son of my grandfather's only son." Who is the man?  
**Solution:** Grandfather's only son = Ravi's father. Son of father = **Ravi's brother** (or Ravi himself).

**Q:** If X + Y means X is father of Y; X − Y means X is mother of Y; X × Y means X is brother of Y. Then P + Q × R − S means?  
**Solution:** P=father of Q. Q=brother of R. R=mother of S. P is **grandfather** of S.

## 4. Practice MCQs

**Q1.** A's mother is B's sister. C is B's mother. D is C's father. How is A related to D?  
A) Granddaughter  B) Grandson  C) Great-granddaughter  D) Cannot determine  
**Answer: D** (Gender of A unknown)

**Q2.** M is N's brother. P is N's father. Q is M's son. How is Q related to P?  
A) Son  B) Grandson  C) Nephew  D) Brother  
**Answer: B**

**Q3.** Introducing a boy, a woman said "He is the only son of my mother's mother." The boy is woman's:  
A) Brother  B) Uncle  C) Cousin  D) Nephew  
**Answer: B** (Mother's mother = grandmother. Her only son = woman's maternal uncle)

**Q4.** A + B means A is father of B. A − B means A is wife of B. P + Q − R means?  
A) P is father-in-law of R  B) P is father of R  C) P is grandfather of R  D) None  
**Answer: A** (P=father of Q. Q=wife of R. P=father-in-law of R)

**Q5.** Pointing to a photo, Arun said "She is the daughter of my father's only son." Who is in photo?  
A) Arun's daughter  B) Arun's sister  C) Arun's mother  D) Arun's niece  
**Answer: A**

**Q6.** B is A's brother. C is A's mother. D is C's father. B is D's?  
A) Grandson  B) Granddaughter  C) Son  D) Cannot determine  
**Answer: A** (B is male → grandson)

**Q7.** If A@B means A is wife of B, A#B means A is son of B, then P@Q#R means P is ___ of R.  
A) Daughter-in-law  B) Wife  C) Mother  D) Sister  
**Answer: A**

**Q8.** X is Y's father. Z is Y's sister. W is Z's son. W is X's?  
A) Son  B) Grandson  C) Nephew  D) Brother  
**Answer: B**

**Q9.** A man said "This girl is the wife of the grandson of my mother." Girl is man's:  
A) Daughter  B) Daughter-in-law  C) Sister  D) Cannot determine  
**Answer: B**

**Q10.** P is Q's brother. R is Q's mother. S is R's father. P is S's?  
A) Grandson  B) Granddaughter  C) Nephew  D) Son  
**Answer: A**

## 5. Exam Strategy

- **Always draw the tree** on paper/scratch pad
- Watch for **gender-neutral** questions ("cannot be determined")
- Coded relations: decode one symbol at a time
- Target time: **45–60 seconds**
